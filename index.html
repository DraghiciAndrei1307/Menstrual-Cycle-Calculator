Menstrual Cycle Overview

The menstrual cycle is a natural, recurring process in a woman's body, marking her reproductive health and fertility. Each cycle typically begins on the first day of menstruation (bleeding) and lasts until the first day of the next menstrual period. While the average cycle is about 28 days, it can vary between 21 to 35 days, depending on individual factors.

Understanding your menstrual cycle is essential, not just for reproductive planning, but also for monitoring overall health and recognizing potential hormonal imbalances. Below is a detailed guide to the menstrual cycle, its phases, and the factors that influence it.
